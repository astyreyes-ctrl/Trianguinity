Formula’s for Coordinate Geometry & Triangles

Quick reference for the formulas used in Trianguinity, with short explanations and worked examples.

Distance Midpoint Slope Line Forms Triangle Area Perimeter Pythagorean / Right Test Isosceles Test Angles (Law of Cosines)

Distance between two points

d(P,Q) = √[(x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)²]

Derived from the Pythagorean theorem on the horizontal and vertical legs between points.

Example: P(−2, 1), Q(3, 5)
d = √[(3 − (−2))² + (5 − 1)²] = √[(5)² + (4)²] = √41 ≈ 6.403

Midpoint

M = ( (x₁ + x₂)/2 , (y₁ + y₂)/2 )

The average of x‑coordinates and y‑coordinates.

Example: A(2, −3), B(6, 5) ⇒ M = ( (2+6)/2 , (−3+5)/2 ) = (4, 1)

Slope

m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)

Rise over run. Undefined when x₂ = x₁ (vertical line).

Example: P(−1, 4), Q(3, 2) ⇒ m = (2 − 4) / (3 − (−1)) = −2 / 4 = −1/2

Equation of a Line (Common Forms)

Slope–intercept: y = mx + b

m is slope, b is y‑intercept.

Point–slope: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)

Through (x₁,y₁) with slope m.

Standard: Ax + By = C

Usually A, B, C integers; B ≥ 0 by convention.

Example: Through (2,1) slope 3 → point–slope: y − 1 = 3(x − 2). Expand: y = 3x − 5 (slope–intercept). Standard: 3x − y = 5.

Area of a Triangle from Coordinates

Area = ½ | x₁(y₂ − y₃) + x₂(y₃ − y₁) + x₃(y₁ − y₂) |

This is the shoelace formula; zero area means the points are collinear (no triangle).

Example: A(0,0), B(4,0), C(1,3) Area = ½ | 0(0 − 3) + 4(3 − 0) + 1(0 − 0) | = ½ · 12 = 6.

Perimeter of a Triangle

P = AB + BC + CA

Compute each side with the distance formula and sum them.

Example: A(0,0), B(3,0), C(0,4) ⇒ AB=3, BC=5, CA=4 → P = 12.

Pythagorean Theorem & Right‑Triangle Test

a² + b² = c²

For sides a ≤ b ≤ c (c is the longest). If a² + b² = c² ⇒ right triangle; < c² ⇒ obtuse; > c² ⇒ acute.

Example: sides 3,4,5 → 3²+4²=9+16=25=5² → right triangle.

Isosceles Test

Triangle is isosceles if at least two sides are equal: AB = BC or BC = CA or CA = AB (within a small measurement tolerance).

Example: If AB = 5, BC = 5, CA = 7 ⇒ isosceles (legs AB and BC).

Angles via Law of Cosines

cos(α) = (b² + c² − a²) / (2bc)
cos(β) = (a² + c² − b²) / (2ac)
cos(γ) = (a² + b² − c²) / (2ab)

Where a, b, c are sides opposite angles α, β, γ respectively.

Example: For sides a=5, b=6, c=7 cos(γ) = (5² + 6² − 7²) / (2·5·6) = (25 + 36 − 49)/60 = 12/60 = 0.2 → γ ≈ 78.46°.
Tip: In the app, toggle Snap to get integer coordinates, which makes distances and areas come out nicely.
